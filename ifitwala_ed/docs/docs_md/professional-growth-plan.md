## What is a Professional Growth Plan?

A **Professional Growth Plan (PGP)** is a structured framework for staff development. It connects goals, stakeholders, review rounds, and ratings into a single growth journey that aligns individual development with school priorities.

<Callout type="info" title="Why Ifitwala Ed is different">
Ifitwala Ed's PGP system is not a one-time appraisal form. It's a living plan with multiple review rounds, stakeholder involvement, and direct linkage to Professional Development requests. Growth becomes continuous, not annual.
</Callout>


## The PGP Ecosystem

The PGP module consists of interconnected components:

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **PGP Template** | Reusable blueprint for creating plans |
| **PGP Plan** | Individual staff member's growth plan |
| **PGP Goal** | Specific development objectives |
| **PGP Rating** | Structured evaluation of goal progress |
| **Rating Scale** | Customizable criteria for evaluations |


## PGP Templates

**Templates** define the structure that individual plans follow. Create a template once, then apply it to many employees.

<Steps title="Creating a PGP Template">
  <Step title="Open Templates">
    Go to **HR > Professional Growth Plan > PGP Template** and click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the Template">
    Enter a descriptive name (e.g., "Annual Teacher Growth Plan").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define Stakeholders">
    In the Stakeholders table, add the roles that should be involved in reviews (e.g., Line Manager, Department Head, Peer).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Review Rounds">
    Define how many review rounds the plan will have (e.g., 3 rounds: mid-term, end-of-year, reflection).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save the template. It is now available for creating plans.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Template reuse">
Create different templates for different staff categories:
- Teaching Staff Growth Plan
- Leadership Growth Plan
- Support Staff Growth Plan
- New Teacher Induction Plan
</Callout>


## PGP Plans

A **PGP Plan** is the individual growth plan assigned to a staff member.

<Steps title="Creating a PGP Plan">
  <Step title="Open Plans">
    Go to **HR > Professional Growth Plan > PGP Plan** and click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Employee">
    Choose the staff member this plan belongs to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Template">
    Select a PGP Template to pre-populate structure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Stakeholders">
    Assign specific people to stakeholder roles from the template.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set State">
    Start as **Draft**, then move to **In Progress** when ready.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save the plan. Goals can now be added.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Plan States

| State | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Draft** | Being prepared, not yet active |
| **In Progress** | Active, goals being worked on |
| **Completed** | All review rounds finished |
| **Archived** | Preserved for historical reference |


## PGP Goals

**Goals** are the specific development objectives within a plan.

<Steps title="Adding Goals to a Plan">
  <Step title="Open the PGP Plan">
    Navigate to the plan and scroll to the Goals section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a Goal">
    Click **Add Row** or go to **HR > Professional Growth Plan > PGP Goal**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the Goal">
    Enter a clear, measurable goal statement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Timeline">
    Define target dates and review milestones.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link to Designation">
    Optionally link goals to specific designations for role-based growth paths.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save the goal. It now appears in the plan's goal list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Goal alignment">
Link PGP Goals to Professional Development requests. When staff request training, selecting a PGP Goal creates clear traceability between growth objectives and learning activities.
</Callout>


## PGP Ratings

**Ratings** evaluate progress on goals during review rounds.

<Steps title="Conducting a Rating Review">
  <Step title="Open Ratings">
    Go to **HR > Professional Growth Plan > PGP Rating** and click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Plan and Goal">
    Link the rating to the specific PGP Plan and PGP Goal being evaluated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Rating Scale">
    Select the appropriate rating scale for this evaluation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Score and Comment">
    Enter scores against each criterion and add qualitative comments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save the rating. The plan's progress updates automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>


## Rating Scales

**Rating Scales** define the criteria and options used for evaluations.

<Steps title="Creating a Rating Scale">
  <Step title="Open Scales">
    Go to **HR > Professional Growth Plan > PGP Rating Scale** and click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the Scale">
    Enter a name (e.g., "Standard Performance Scale").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define Options">
    In the Rating Scale Options table, add each level with a label and numeric value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save the scale. It is now available for ratings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Scale examples">
Common rating scales include:
- Exceeds Expectations / Meets Expectations / Developing / Needs Support
- Outstanding / Good / Satisfactory / Unsatisfactory
- Custom scales for specific competencies
</Callout>


## Review Rounds

PGP Plans support multiple **review rounds** for continuous feedback:

1. **Round 1** — Mid-term check-in
2. **Round 2** — End-of-cycle evaluation
3. **Round 3** — Reflection and forward planning

<Callout type="tip" title="Round tracking">
The PGP Plan tracks:
- Total rounds expected (from template)
- Rounds completed so far
- Overall progress percentage
</Callout>


## Linking PD to Growth Plans

The power of PGP comes from connecting growth plans to actual professional development:

<Steps title="Connecting PD to a PGP Goal">
  <Step title="Submit PD Request">
    When creating a Professional Development Request, scroll to the PGP Alignment section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Plan">
    Choose your active PGP Plan.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Goal">
    Choose the specific PGP Goal this training supports.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Save the request. The linkage is now visible in both the PD record and the PGP Plan.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

### For Employees
- Set 3-5 focused goals per plan cycle
- Make goals specific and measurable
- Update progress regularly
- Link PD requests to relevant goals
- Prepare evidence before review rounds

### For Managers
- Schedule review rounds in advance
- Provide specific, actionable feedback in ratings
- Use consistent rating scales across the team
- Align individual goals with department priorities

### For HR
- Create standardized templates for consistency
- Train managers on rating scale usage
- Monitor completion rates across the organization
- Archive completed plans for historical reference

## Common Questions

**Q: How many goals should a plan have?**
A: 3-5 well-defined goals is typical. Too many goals dilute focus.

**Q: Can I change goals after the plan is in progress?**
A: Goals can typically be refined, but major changes should be documented. Check your organization's policy.

**Q: What if a staff member has no template?**
A: Create a plan without a template, or ask HR to create an appropriate template first.

**Q: How do review rounds work?**
A: Each round is an evaluation point. Stakeholders rate goal progress, and the plan tracks how many rounds are complete.

**Q: Can goals span multiple academic years?**
A: PGP Plans are typically scoped to an academic year. Multi-year goals should be broken into annual milestones.

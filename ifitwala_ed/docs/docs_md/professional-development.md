## What is Professional Development?

**Professional Development (PD)** in Ifitwala Ed is a closed-loop workflow for managing staff training, conferences, certifications, and growth activities. It connects requests, budgets, records, outcomes, and accounting in one governed process.

<Callout type="info" title="Why Ifitwala Ed is different">
Unlike simple reimbursement forms, Ifitwala Ed's PD workflow enforces budget reservation before approval, tracks completion evidence, and links activities to Professional Growth Plans. It's governance that ensures every training dollar is well spent.
</Callout>

---

## The PD Lifecycle

The Professional Development workflow follows a clear path:

```
Request → Review → Approve → Record → Complete → Outcome → Liquidate
```

| Stage | DocType | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Request** | Professional Development Request | Staff propose training activities |
| **Review** | Same request | Managers and HR evaluate against budget and goals |
| **Approve** | Same request | Authorized roles grant approval |
| **Record** | Professional Development Record | Committed operational truth created |
| **Complete** | Same record | Staff mark attendance and submit evidence |
| **Outcome** | Professional Development Outcome | Completion reflection and actual costs |
| **Liquidate** | PD Encumbrance | Accounting finalization |

## Professional Development Themes

**Themes** define the strategic focus areas for professional development.

<Steps title="Creating a PD Theme">
  <Step title="Open Themes">
    Go to **HR > Professional Development > Professional Development Theme**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Details">
    Enter the theme name, organization, and description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Scope">
    Leave School blank for organization-wide themes, or select a specific school.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate">
    Check **Is Active** and save.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Theme examples">
- Instructional Technology
- Differentiated Learning
- Safeguarding and Child Protection
- Leadership Development
- Subject-Specific Pedagogy
</Callout>

## Professional Development Budgets

**Budgets** control how much can be spent on professional development. Each budget represents a reservable pool.

<Steps title="Setting Up a PD Budget">
  <Step title="Open Budgets">
    Go to **HR > Professional Development > Professional Development Budget**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define Scope">
    Select organization, school, and academic year.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Budget Mode">
    Select the pool type: School Pool, Employee Allowance, Department Pool, Program Pool, or Hybrid.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Targeting">
    Depending on mode, select employee, department, or program.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Finance">
    Set the encumbrance, expense, and clearing accounts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Amount">
    Set the total budget amount. Reserved and liquidated amounts calculate automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate">
    Check **Is Active** and save.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Budget Modes Explained

| Mode | Targeting | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **School Pool** | No targeting required | General school PD fund |
| **Employee Allowance** | Per employee | Individual training budgets |
| **Department Pool** | Per department | Department-specific training |
| **Program Pool** | Per program | Program-wide initiatives |
| **Hybrid** | Flexible | Mixed-use funds |

<Callout type="warning" title="Budget invariants">
- Available amount = Budget - Reserved - Liquidated
- Inactive budgets cannot accept new reservations
- Budget scope must exactly match request scope on organization, school, and academic year
</Callout>

## Submitting a PD Request

Staff members initiate professional development through a **PD Request**.

<Steps title="Requesting Professional Development">
  <Step title="Open Requests">
    Go to **HR > Professional Development > Professional Development Request** and click **New**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Scope">
    Confirm your employee, organization, school, and academic year.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Budget">
    Choose the PD budget that covers this request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link to Growth Plan">
    Optionally select a PGP Plan and PGP Goal to align this PD with your growth objectives.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the Activity">
    Enter title, type (Conference, Workshop, Certification, etc.), provider, location, dates, and absence days.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Costs">
    In the Costs table, add each expense item (registration, travel, accommodation, etc.). The estimated total calculates automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Save and submit the request. It moves to **Submitted** status and enters the review queue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Request Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | Working on the request; not yet submitted |
| **Submitted** | Sent for review |
| **Under Review** | Being evaluated by approvers |
| **Approved** | Granted; record and encumbrance created |
| **Rejected** | Denied; reason should be provided |
| **Cancelled** | Withdrawn by the employee |

<Callout type="info" title="Duplicate protection">
The system blocks duplicate active requests for the same employee, academic year, title, and start date.
</Callout>

## Reviewing and Approving PD Requests

HR and managers review submitted requests against budget availability and strategic alignment.

<Steps title="Approving a PD Request">
  <Step title="View Submissions">
    Go to **HR > Professional Development > Professional Development Request** and filter by status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Details">
    Open the request and check the budget snapshot, estimated costs, and PGP alignment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Validation">
    Review the validation status. If blocked, an override may be needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decide">
    Change the status to **Approved** or **Rejected**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save the decision. If approved, a PD Record and Encumbrance are created automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="What happens on approval">
When a PD request is approved:
- A **Professional Development Record** is created
- A **Professional Development Encumbrance** reserves the budget
- An **Employee Booking** blocks the absence days
- The budget's reserved amount increases
</Callout>

## Professional Development Records

A **PD Record** is the committed operational truth. It tracks the activity from approval through completion.

### Record Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Planned** | Approved, awaiting the event |
| **Attended** | Staff attended the activity |
| **Completed** | Finished, awaiting outcome |
| **Cancelled** | Activity cancelled; budget released |
| **Liquidated** | Fully closed with actual costs |

<Callout type="tip" title="Cancelling a record">
Cancelling a planned or completed record releases both the employee booking and the budget reservation. This is useful when events are cancelled or staff cannot attend.
</Callout>

## Recording Outcomes

After attending professional development, staff submit an **Outcome** to document what was learned and actual costs incurred.

<Steps title="Submitting a PD Outcome">
  <Step title="Open the PD Record">
    Navigate to your PD Record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create Outcome">
    Go to **HR > Professional Development > Professional Development Outcome** and link it to your record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Completion Details">
    Enter completion date, reflection on learning, and actual total cost.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach Evidence">
    Upload certificates, attendance proofs, or other evidence in the Evidence table.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark Ready">
    Check **Liquidation Ready** when complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Evidence requirement">
Your organization may require completion evidence before liquidation. This is controlled by **HR Settings > PD Require Completion Evidence**.
</Callout>

## Liquidation

**Liquidation** is the accounting finalization step. It converts the budget reservation into actual recorded expenses.

<Callout type="warning" title="Finance access">
Liquidation involves accounting entries. Accounts Manager or Accounts User roles can view encumbrances. HR and finance teams coordinate to complete this step.
</Callout>

### Encumbrance Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | Initial state |
| **Reserved** | Budget blocked by approved request |
| **Released** | Reservation reversed (e.g., cancellation) |
| **Liquidated** | Finalized with actual expenses |

## Best Practices

### For Employees
- Align PD requests with your PGP goals
- Submit requests well in advance of event dates
- Include all anticipated costs in the request
- Submit outcomes promptly after attendance
- Attach clear evidence for verification

### For Managers and HR
- Review requests against strategic themes
- Ensure budget availability before approving
- Check for duplicate or overlapping requests
- Follow up on outstanding outcomes
- Coordinate with finance for timely liquidation

### For Finance
- Verify actual costs match or are explained against estimates
- Process liquidation only when evidence is complete
- Monitor encumbrance balances for period-end reporting

<DoDont doTitle="Do" dontTitle="Don't">
  <Do>Link PD requests to PGP goals for strategic alignment.</Do>
  <Do>Submit requests early to allow review time.</Do>
  <Do>Include all cost items in the initial request.</Do>
  <Do>Submit outcomes and evidence promptly after training.</Do>
  <Dont>Approve requests without checking budget availability.</Dont>
  <Dont>Forget to cancel records when events are called off.</Dont>
  <Dont>Liquidate without required evidence.</Dont>
</DoDont>

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I request PD without a budget?**
A: No. Every request must link to an active PD budget with available funds.

**Q: What happens if actual costs exceed the estimate?**
A: The outcome captures actual costs. If significantly over budget, an override may be required before liquidation.

**Q: Can I cancel PD after approval?**
A: Yes. Cancelling the PD Record releases the booking and budget reservation.

**Q: How does PD link to my growth plan?**
A: When submitting a request, select your PGP Plan and a specific PGP Goal. This creates traceability between your growth objectives and training activities.

**Q: What evidence is required for completion?**
A: This depends on your organization's HR Settings. Common evidence includes certificates, attendance confirmations, invoices, and written reflections.
